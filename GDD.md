# The Bean Farmer

## Overview

The Bean Farmer is a 3D farming and progression game centered on growing, harvesting, and selling many different kinds of beans. The player starts with a small bean plot and gradually builds a massive bean farm by unlocking new bean varieties, expanding farmland, completing quests, and investing in player-focused upgrades.

The game should feel cozy at first, then steadily open into a broader management and collection loop. Its core appeal is simple: plant beans, optimize output, discover rare varieties, and grow a small patch into a specialized bean empire.

## Core Pitch

Plant beans, grow beans, sell beans, buy more land, unlock rare beans, and become the greatest bean farmer around.

## Genre

- 3D farming / life-sim
- Collection and progression game
- Single-player

## Design Goals

1. Make farming immediately understandable and satisfying.
2. Give players a large set of bean types to discover and master.
3. Create a strong sense of expansion from tiny plot to giant farm.
4. Support short relaxing sessions and longer optimization sessions.
5. Reward progression with both practical upgrades and collectible milestones.

## Player Fantasy

The player is not just running a farm. They are building a bean-focused legacy, learning the quirks of each bean type, expanding into new land, and unlocking rare rewards that make them feel like a true master farmer.

## Core Gameplay Loop

1. Prepare soil and plant beans.
2. Water, maintain, and wait for growth.
3. Harvest mature crops.
4. Sell beans for profit.
5. Use earnings to buy seeds, tools, upgrades, and new land.
6. Unlock new bean varieties and pursue rare beans.
7. Complete quests and side activities for bonus rewards.
8. Repeat with bigger yields and more efficient farm layouts.

## Setting

The game takes place in a stylized farming world focused on bean agriculture. The initial setting is a modest starter farm, but the world expands into larger fields, specialty plots, quest locations, and challenge spaces as the player progresses.

The tone should be light, charming, and a little playful. The concept is specific enough to benefit from humor and personality, but the game still needs strong progression depth to keep it from feeling like a joke premise.

## Game Structure

### Starting State

- The player begins with a small starter field.
- Only basic bean types are available.
- Tools and movement are simple and limited.
- Money is tight, so early decisions matter.

### Midgame

- The player unlocks more bean varieties.
- Land expansion becomes a major goal.
- Quests and mini-games provide alternate progression paths.
- The bean index starts to matter as a collection and mastery system.

### Endgame

- The player operates a large, highly optimized farm.
- Rare or special beans become the main chase.
- Upgrades reshape efficiency, harvest speed, and profits.
- Challenges and completion goals drive long-term engagement.

## Bean System

Beans are the heart of the game. Different bean types should not just be visual swaps. They should differ in one or more of the following:

- Growth time
- Sell value
- Yield amount
- Seasonal preference
- Soil preference
- Water needs
- Rarity
- Mutation or hybrid potential
- Quest relevance

### Bean Categories

- Common beans: Fast to understand, cheap to plant, reliable profit.
- Advanced beans: Better value but more demanding care or longer growth.
- Rare beans: Harder to obtain, used for major profits, milestones, or special rewards.
- Special beans: Reward-track beans linked to quests, challenges, or unique mechanics.

### Bean Discovery

New bean types can be unlocked through:

- Purchasing better seeds
- Reaching sales milestones
- Expanding to new land
- Completing quests
- Winning mini-games or challenges
- Discovering hidden or rare seeds

## Farming Systems

### Planting

- Players select a bean seed and place it in prepared soil.
- Different land plots may support different efficiencies.

### Growth

- Beans grow over time across clear visual stages.
- Growth can be influenced by upgrades, conditions, and bean traits.

### Harvesting

- Mature beans can be harvested manually at first.
- Later upgrades may improve harvest speed, yield, or automation support.

### Selling

- Beans are sold for currency.
- Market values may be static in the first version, with optional dynamic pricing in later updates.

### Expansion

- Selling crops funds land purchases.
- New land unlocks more planting capacity and potentially new gameplay opportunities.

## Land and Farm Growth

Land expansion is one of the main long-term motivators. The player should visibly transform the farm over time.

Possible expansion layers:

- More planting plots
- Better irrigation zones
- Premium soil sections
- Decorative or prestige areas
- Dedicated rare-bean zones
- Utility buildings for processing or storage

The farm itself should become a visual trophy of progression.

## Progression Systems

### Money Progression

- Sell harvested beans
- Reinvest in tools, seeds, and land
- Increase income through better crops and upgrades

### Unlock Progression

- More bean types
- Bigger farm area
- Better movement or farming efficiency
- Access to challenges and side systems

### Mastery Progression

- Track discovered bean types
- Complete the bean index
- Improve knowledge of best growth strategies

## Bean Index

The Bean Index is a collection and information system that records every bean the player discovers.

Each entry can include:

- Bean name
- Rarity
- Sell value
- Growth time
- Preferred conditions
- Best use
- Whether the player has fully mastered it

The index should work as both a completion system and a practical strategy guide.

## Quests

Quests give the player structure beyond pure farming profit. They should introduce goals, reward exploration, and create reasons to grow specific beans.

Quest examples:

- Grow a certain number of a bean type
- Deliver a rare bean
- Reach a sales target
- Unlock a new plot
- Harvest perfect-quality crops
- Discover a hidden bean variety

Quest rewards can include:

- Currency
- Seeds
- Rare beans
- Cosmetics
- Upgrade materials
- Exclusive challenge access

## Mini-Games and Challenges

The notes call for simple mini-games, including:

- Mining
- Bean Index work
- Challenges
- Quests

### Mining

Mining can act as a side activity for upgrade resources, rare seed materials, or currency supplements. It gives the player something different to do while keeping the core progression loop moving.

### Challenges

Challenges can test efficiency or specialization, such as:

- Grow a target yield within a time limit
- Earn a target profit in one day
- Harvest with minimal wasted water
- Grow difficult beans under bad conditions

Challenges are a good source for special rewards and long-term mastery goals.

## Rewards and Special Beans

Special beans are an important part of the concept. They should feel distinct from normal crops and tie directly into rewards.

Possible uses for special beans:

- High-value sales
- Quest turn-ins
- Unlocking exclusive upgrades
- Prestige progression
- Cosmetic rewards
- Access to new challenge tiers

This creates a strong loop where farming skill directly supports character and farm improvement.

## Player Upgrades

The notes specifically mention player upgrades. That implies progression should not be limited to the farm itself.

Possible player upgrade categories:

- Faster movement
- Faster planting
- Faster harvesting
- Larger carry capacity
- Better stamina or action efficiency
- Improved rare-seed discovery chance
- Better sale bonuses
- Better tool handling

This helps the player feel personally stronger, not just richer.

## Farm Upgrades

To complement player upgrades, farm upgrades should include:

- Better tools
- Improved watering systems
- Better soil quality
- Storage improvements
- Growth boosters
- Harvest efficiency upgrades
- Expansion discounts

## 3D World Direction

The notes explicitly say the game must be 3D.

That means the game should use:

- A readable third-person or slightly elevated farm view
- Distinct plot layouts and visible crop growth
- A world that feels pleasant to walk through
- Clear silhouettes for beans at each growth stage

The 3D style should aim for clarity over realism. A stylized look will fit the concept better than a highly realistic one.

## Inspiration Direction

The notes describe the game as similar in feel to a Roblox-style farming game structure, with side activities and reward loops. The intended takeaway is not to copy a specific title directly, but to match the accessible, progression-heavy structure:

- Simple actions
- Frequent rewards
- constant unlocking
- Visible farm growth
- Collection goals
- Side content beyond farming

## Camera and Controls

Recommended approach:

- Third-person movement in the farm world
- Simple interaction prompts for planting, watering, harvesting, selling, and quest turn-ins
- Fast UI access to seeds, index, upgrades, and active quests

## UI and UX

Key UI surfaces:

- Money total
- Current seeds / inventory
- Active quest tracker
- Bean Index menu
- Upgrade menu
- Farm map or land ownership view
- Challenge menu

UX priorities:

- Farming actions must be fast
- Bean data must be easy to compare
- Rewards should be frequent and clearly communicated
- Expansion opportunities should always feel visible

## Art Direction

- Bright, readable, stylized 3D visuals
- Appealing farmland shapes and strong color coding by bean type
- Beans should be visually collectible and easy to identify
- Rare and special beans should feel immediately distinct
- The farm should become more impressive as it expands

## Audio Direction

The game should use warm, playful farm audio with satisfying feedback for:

- Planting
- Watering
- Harvesting
- Selling
- Unlocking new beans
- Completing quests
- Receiving rare rewards

## Progression Retention Hooks

- Discover every bean type
- Fully upgrade the player
- Expand the full farm
- Complete all quests
- Beat all challenge tiers
- Collect all special beans
- Finish the Bean Index

## Scope Recommendation

### Vertical Slice

For a first playable version:

- 1 starter farm map
- 6 to 10 bean types
- Basic plant/grow/harvest/sell loop
- 1 land expansion path
- 1 mini-game, likely mining
- Basic quest system
- Bean Index
- Simple player and farm upgrades

### Full Version

- Large farm expansion tree
- Many bean types
- Rare and special beans
- More mini-games and challenge modes
- Deeper upgrade systems
- More areas and stronger late-game mastery loops

## Risks

- The joke strength of the bean theme could overshadow depth if progression is too shallow.
- Too many bean types without meaningful differences would create fake variety.
- Mini-games could distract from the farming loop if they are not tied tightly to progression.
- A 3D game can become expensive quickly if the scope is not controlled.
- If upgrades are too linear, the game may lose replay value.

## Open Design Questions

- Are seasons part of the design, or is farming all-season?
- Is there a day/night system?
- Are beans processed into products, or only sold raw?
- Should special beans come from mutation, quests, or both?
- Is the game purely offline single-player, or eventually social/multiplayer?
- How much automation should late-game players unlock?
